# Engage Island — Guide Voice Direction Sheet

One page for casting + directing the nine guide voices (ElevenLabs or studio).
Audience: children ~5–12 in a therapeutic app. Every voice must feel **safe,
warm, and unhurried** — expressive but never loud, zany-but-gentle at most.
No sarcasm, no edge, no adult irony. Think "trusted camp counselor," never
"Saturday-morning commercial."

**Global technical spec:** mp3, mono, 64–96 kbps · recorded at natural
"reading to one child" distance · room-dry, no reverb · files keyed to
content IDs per `content/README.md` (never renumber) · leave ~0.2 s head/tail
silence · target loudness consistent across all nine (normalize to about
−19 LUFS mono).

| Guide | Zone | Character | Voice age/quality | Pace | Direction notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain Pete** (Pelican) | Welcome Dock | Weathered, jolly harbor captain; the first voice every child hears | Adult male-ish, warm gravel, a smile in it | Medium, hearty | Big welcome without booming. "Ahoy!" is friendly, not theatrical pirate. He makes arrival feel celebrated and safe. |
| **Shelly** (Turtle) | Calm Beach | The slow-breathing regulator; leads Wave Breathing | Older, soft, androgynous-leaning-female | **Slowest of all nine** | Elongated vowels, audible gentle exhales welcome ("Heyyy… no rush here…"). Her practice lines pace the child's breath: leave real pauses. Never sleepy — serene. |
| **Olive** (Owl) | Treehouse Hideaway | Whimsical imagination-keeper, a little bookish | Female, light, twinkly | Medium-slow | Playful "whooo" wordplay lands soft, not hammy. Sense of wonder — she's inviting the child UP into make-believe. |
| **Wally** (Whale) | Lighthouse Point | The deep-breath thinker; quiet feelings zone | Deep, resonant, slow male | Slow | Lowest voice in the cast. Spacious, contemplative; "it's nice to just think here" should feel like permission. Never mournful. |
| **Rascal** (Raccoon) | Star Market | Cheeky little shopkeeper, delighted by shiny things | Bright, quick, kid-adjacent (any gender) | Medium-fast | The most mischievous — but sweet mischief. Conspiratorial excitement ("Ooh, a visitor!"), like sharing a secret treasure. |
| **Mango** (Monkey) | Arcade Cove | The energy kid; games and play | Young, bouncy, higher pitch | **Fastest of all nine** | Genuine giggle-adjacent energy, exclamation points earned. Still caps at "excited friend," never shrill or overwhelming. |
| **Fern** (Fox) | Art Hut | Encouraging maker; creative confidence | Warm female, gentle brightness | Medium | The "there's no wrong way to make art" voice. Curious and affirming; delight at whatever the child makes. |
| **Bruno** (Bear) | Campfire Circle | Big cozy protector; stories and belonging | Big, soft male — a bear hug in audio | Medium-slow | Low warmth, slight smile. "Pull up a log" should feel like the safest seat in the world. Steady — he's the grounding presence. |
| **Finn** (Frog) | Lazy Lagoon | Laid-back chill friend; permission to do nothing | Relaxed, mid-pitch, any gender | Slow, loose | Almost drawled, sunny. "Ribbit!" is a happy verbal shrug, not a gag. The anti-pressure voice. |

**Casting contrast check** (so the nine stay distinguishable with eyes closed):
Wally deepest → Bruno big-warm → Captain Pete gravel-warm → Shelly softest/slowest
→ Finn loose → Fern warm-mid → Olive light → Rascal bright-quick → Mango youngest/fastest.

**Priority order for recording** (matches what ships first — see the dev
missing-audio checklist for exact line IDs): 1) Captain Pete greeting
(auto-plays on every arrival), 2) Shelly greeting + Wave Breathing intro +
steps (the only wired practice), 3) the other seven greetings.
